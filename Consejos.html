RECOMMENDATIONS

Do you want to learn how to make the most of our smart trash bin?

Here we present some recommendations and tips to get the best use out of our bin, allowing you to produce 100% organic compost in approximately 15 days.

What to Use?

Organic Waste Options to Use

Essentially, seeds, vegetables, and fruits are good ingredients for quality compost. However, it is important to know that each food item may have a more specific result.

Recommended to Use

  • Fruit and vegetable scraps.
  • Crushed eggshells.
  • Coffee grounds and filters.
  • Dry leaves and cut grass.
  • Cardboard without ink and plain paper.

Use in Moderation

  • Citrus peels in excess.
  • Bread, rice, or pasta (small amounts).
  • Banana peels (cut).
  • Lightly cooked food scraps.
  • Old bread or plain cookies.

Do Not Use

  • Meat and bones.
  • Oils and fats.
  • Dairy products.
  • Pet waste.
  • Plastics, metals, glass.

What to Avoid?

  • Dairy: Has a very high pH which causes loss of essential nutrients like nitrogen, and makes it harder for plants to absorb nutrients.
  • Citrus: Makes the compost pH too acidic and low, slowing down the composting process.
